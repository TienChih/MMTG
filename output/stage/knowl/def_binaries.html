Definition 2.7.2.

A magma is an algebraic structure with a single binary operation.
In other words, a magma \((S,\cdot)\) consists of a set \(S\) of elements, together with a binary operation \(\cdot \colon S\times S \to S\) that assigns to every “input” pair of elements of \(S\text{,}\) an “output” element of \(S\text{.}\)
