Exercise 1.

Consider the magma whose elements are integers, \(\mathbb{Z} = \{ \ldots, -3,-2,-1,0,1,2,3,\ldots,\}\text{,}\) and whose binary operation called \(\triangleright\) is defined by
\begin{equation*} x \triangleright y = 2y - x. \end{equation*}
  1. Show that \((\mathbb{Z},\triangleright)\) has the idempotent property.
  2. Show that \((\mathbb{Z},\triangleright)\) has the right-distributive property. Does it also have the left-distributive property?
  3. Simplify the expression \(\bigl( x\triangleright y\bigr) \triangleright y\text{.}\) What does this result tell you about whether this magma is associative?
  4. Does \((\mathbb{Z},\triangleright)\) have the identity property? How can you tell?
